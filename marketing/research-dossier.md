# Partners in Biz — Research Dossier

## Client Overview

**Partners in Biz** is a B2B SaaS client growth platform built by Peet Stander. It combines AI-powered agent orchestration (Hermes), multi-platform social media automation, CRM with world-class features, paid media management, SEO sprints, and client document workflow into a single integrated platform.

## Target Audiences

### Primary: Agency Owners & Boutique Service Businesses
- Digital marketing agencies looking to scale operations
- Web development studios needing client management
- Design agencies wanting integrated workflows
- Small consulting firms (2-20 people)

**Pain points:**
- Fragmented tool stack (social scheduler, CRM, project management, analytics all separate)
- Time wasted on manual content publishing across platforms
- No unified view of client growth and campaign performance
- Can't prove ROI to clients with scattered data

### Secondary: High-Growth SMEs
- E-commerce brands managing their own marketing
- B2B SaaS companies with in-house growth teams
- Professional services firms (legal, accounting, financial)

**Pain points:**
- Need to publish to multiple platforms but lack time
- Want better analytics attribution
- Need AI assistance for content creation
- Require client collaboration on campaigns

### Tertiary: Marketing Teams in Larger Organizations
- In-house marketing teams at mid-sized companies
- Agencies serving enterprise clients
- Growth marketing teams

**Pain points:**
- Need approval workflows for content
- Require multi-platform consistency
- Want AI-generated content at scale
- Need enterprise-grade features (permissions, audit trails)

## Brand Positioning

### Current Brand Voice (from brand guidelines)
- **Tone**: Professional, confident, technically competent, growth-focused
- **Voice**: Direct, no fluff, numbers-driven, respectful of time
- **Values**: Transparency, client success, operational excellence, AI empowerment
- **Differentiation**: "AI-first approach + world-class CRM + client collaboration" — not just another scheduler

### Key Differentiators
1. **AI Agent Orchestration** — Hermes agents handle research, content creation, SEO, and outreach
2. **Unified Platform** — Social, CRM, Projects, Analytics, Ads, Email in one place
3. **Client Collaboration** — Real-time campaign reviews, approvals, and feedback loops
4. **Enterprise-Grade** — Proper permissions, audit trails, security, compliance
5. **South African Roots** — Built for ZAR pricing, local support, regional context

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors
- **Buffer/Hootsuite** — Social scheduling only, no CRM or AI
- **HubSpot** — Enterprise pricing, complex onboarding, US-centric
- **Mailchimp/ActiveCampaign** — Email focus, limited social publishing
- **Zoho/Salesforce** — CRM giants, marketing module is an afterthought

### Indirect Competitors
- Agency workflow tools (Asana, Monday.com, ClickUp)
- CMS platforms (WordPress, Webflow) with plugin ecosystems
- Individual specialist tools (Canva, Later, Sprout Social)

### PiB's Competitive Advantages
- All-in-one vs fragmented stack
- AI agents reduce manual work by 70-80%
- Client collaboration layer built-in
- South African pricing (more affordable than US SaaS)
- Faster onboarding (hours vs weeks)

## Channel Mix Strategy

Based on B2B SaaS best practices and target audience behavior:

### Primary Channel: LinkedIn (60% of effort)
- **Why**: Decision-makers (agency owners, marketing directors) are active daily
- **Content types**: Thought leadership, case studies, ROI metrics, integrations
- **Format**: Long-form posts (1,500+ words), carousels, native video
- **Cadence**: 3-4 posts per week

### Secondary Channels (combined 40%):
- **Twitter/X** (15%): Quick tips, AI use cases, platform updates
- **Facebook** (15%): Community building, team transparency, behind-the-scenes
- **Instagram** (10%): Visual storytelling, product screenshots, team culture

### Future Channels (low priority now):
- TikTok/Reels (requires more video production capacity)
- YouTube (long-form tutorials, but LinkedIn native video is better ROI right now)

## Current Website & Brand Analysis

From `/var/lib/hermes/cowork-wiki/agents/partners/wiki/brand-guidelines.md`:

**Palette:**
- Primary background: `#0A0A0B` (near-black with layered tones for depth)
- Primary accent: `#F5A623` (golden amber)
- Secondary accent: `#D4A85D` (warmer gold)
- Alert (rare use): `#E07070` (soft red)
- Text primary: `#FFFFFF` (white on dark)
- Text muted: `#A0A0A0` (gray for secondary text)

**Typography:**
- Headings: Instrument Serif (elegant, premium feel)
- Body: Inter (clean, highly legible)
- Numeric: Inter Mono (for data, metrics, code)
- Fallback: system-ui (Graceful degradation)

**Aesthetic Keywords:**
- Premium but not luxury
- Technical but not cold
- Dark-mode-first (stunning on OLED, high contrast)
- No clichés (no stock photos of handshakes, suits pointing at cameras)
- Data-viz friendly (charts, dashboards, metrics prominent)

**Tone Guidelines:**
- DO: Respect time, quantify everything, cite specific outcomes
- DON'T: Use vague hype, talk down, pretend it's a no-brainer
- Target: Stripe blog readability — smart, dense, never wasteful

## Content Risks to Avoid

1. **Over-promising AI** — Be realistic about what agents can/can't do
2. **Comparing to enterprise SaaS** — Different audience, don't alienate
3. **Neglecting the human element** — AI is a tool, humans still make decisions
4. **US-centric pricing/language** — Keep South African context visible
5. **Technical jargon without explanation** — B2B buyers are busy, not all technical
